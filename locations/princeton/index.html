We Buy Mobile Homes in Princeton, Texas

Iron Lot Homes is the trusted mobile home buyer serving Princeton, TX and the eastern reaches of Collin County. Whether your manufactured home sits on a rural lot along the US-380 corridor, in one of the area's established mobile home parks, or on agricultural land that has been in your family for generations, we are ready to make you a fair cash offer.

Princeton has long been known as a small, tight-knit community with deep agricultural roots. Located east of McKinney along US-380, this once-quiet town has experienced explosive growth over the past decade. New housing developments, schools, and commercial centers are transforming Princeton from a rural farming community into a thriving suburb of the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex.

Why Princeton Mobile Home Owners Trust Iron Lot Homes

The rapid development sweeping through Princeton presents both opportunities and uncertainties for manufactured home owners. As new subdivisions replace open farmland, mobile home communities in the area face changing dynamics. Some residents want to cash out while their property is still valuable. Others need to relocate as the community around them transforms.

Iron Lot Homes understands the unique Princeton market. We know that many manufactured homes in this area sit on private land rather than in traditional parks. We understand the agricultural exemption implications, the title transfer process for homes on acreage, and the specific challenges that come with selling a mobile home in a rapidly developing rural area.

Princeton's Manufactured Housing Landscape

Princeton's affordable land prices have historically made it a haven for manufactured housing. Families who wanted space, fresh air, and proximity to the DFW job market without the high costs of McKinney or Plano found their answer in Princeton. Single-wide and double-wide manufactured homes on half-acre to multi-acre lots are common throughout the community.

The US-380 corridor that runs through Princeton connects the town to McKinney to the west and Farmersville to the east. Along this corridor and the surrounding county roads, you will find a diverse mix of manufactured home communities, individual homes on private lots, and family properties that have housed multiple generations.
